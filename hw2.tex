\begin{problem}[Exercise 4.18.]
Let $A$ and $B$ be sets. Assume $A$ is infinite, $B$ is countable, and $A$ and $B$ are disjoint. Prove $A \sim A \cup B.$ Hint: The strategy of Theorem 4.16 may be useful. 
\end{problem}


\begin{solution}
If $A$ is infinite, we have $C\subset A$, a countably infinite set. The union of two countable sets is still countable, $B\cup C$, which is countably infinite. Since $((A\cup B)\setminus B\cup C) \cap C$ and $((A\cup B)\setminus B\cup C) \cap (B\cup C)$ are both empty, $A \cup B = ((A\cup B)\setminus B\cup C) \cup (B\cup C) \sim ((A\cup B)\setminus B\cup C) \cup C = A.$
\end{solution}
